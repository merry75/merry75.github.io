About Me

I am from Paris and I recently made my allyah. Since then alot of things happened.
I switched from the online markekting industry to the high tech industry.
When I think about it, doing my allyah was the trigger pushing me into coding. I mean dont get me wrong I always wanted to learn how to code, I just never felt like it was the time for me to do it but getting there in Israel and struggling to find my way at the beginning, adapting to the change of this new country, I kind of felt like it was the moment to jump into it.
Since then, there hasn't been a day where I regret it. To me coding is a state of transition from what you can imagine to making it happen in the real world. I'm excited to actually learn new technologies and getting better at what I do every day. And what makes me happy is that I know it's a never ending process as you can never truly master everything so to speak.
I started by teaching myself by getting into tutorials after tutorials, then I stumbled onto this amazing self teaching website freecodecamp which was truly one of the best resource i came across as a beginner. After a few months, I had a pretty good idea to know this is what I wanted to do from now on and so I joined a coding bootcamp in Tel Aviv at Elevation academy.
A 3 month intensive course including html,css,javascript and the MEAN stack. Since then, I keep honing my skills every week, working on projects, side-projects, doing some hackatons and I love it.
I did not find a job in development role and accepted a position in checkpoint as QA engineer and ended up in the automation testing team. It strengthened my core knowledge in cyber-security (automation system testing, network security, jsystem, linux, jira and so on.
A year has passed, and I left checkpoint because I needed time to build a crypto hedge fund that is now running smoothly on the side and I am now looking to kickstart my career in full stack web development. The only thing missing to me right now is to find a job so I can learn from experienced people in the Industry and keep getting better at it. It would be really cool if you guys had an opportunity for me.
